Below is some info about my format and my standard questions so you don't go in blind.  Here is a link to schedule a time with me:
http://www.meetme.so/DevOnFire
This will book a 1 hour time slot - I expect our interview to take 35-45 minutes, but like to allot an hour so we can chat a bit before and/or after.
Please also give me these things:
  • Please send me a contact request on Skype - I am raelyard on Skype - or share your Skype id with me.
  • Also, Please send me a short bio that describes who you are and what you do and a photo (ideally a headshot) of yourself to include in my show notes (you can just give me a link to it and I'll download).
  • Also also, Please make sure you have headphones you can use for the call. If you are using speakers, my sound will feed back into your microphone and impair the pristine recordings I have of both sides.
  • Also also also, I will want a local recording of your audio so I don't have to trust what I receive from Skype as your end of the conversation.  There's a great product to facilitate this, so you don't need to install anything (unless you don't have a modern browser).  You'll only need to visit a link in your browser (Chrome or Firebox, please) and authorize your browser to use your microphone.  I'll give you the link when we record.
Podcast information:
Developer On Fire with Dave Rael is an interview podcast with inspiring and successful software professionals telling personal stories about their experiences with delivering value.  It is a chance for you to get to know your favorite geeks.
Standard questions:
  • What lights you up about technology and what you are currently doing?
  • How did you get started writing software?
  • Please share a story about time you failed.
  • Please share the story of your greatest success.  Make sure you share how this experience delivered value.
  • How do you stay current with what you need to know?
  • Book Recommendation
  • What has you the most excited about your current projects and future?
  • What are the greatest sources of pain in your life and work?
  • What do you geek out about other than software that really lights you up?
  • Can you make one prediction for the future of software?
  • 3 tips for delivering more value - they don't have to be programming or software related, but should facilitate delivering.
